Level 1 Courses

Level 1 courses introduce students to the fundamentals of coding through a variety of applications in mathematics, science, and the arts. These courses take a an exploratory and project-based approach. Students will learn important computational thinking skills that will prepare them for both Level 2 courses and further studies in other disciplines.

Creative Coding

½ Credit

Students in this course will investigate basic computer science concepts and learn how to program by making art. No previous programming experience is necessary. We will use Processing language to create interactive drawings and animations. We will use Arduino-based microcomputers, sensors, LEDs, cardboard, and other art supplies to create collaborative and individual projects that require participation from the audience. Possibilities include rovers, a petting zoo, arcade games, masks, puppets, a parade, and more. Students will follow an iterative design process to create three or four major projects per semester. Students will be assessed on their growth as programmers and makers, their artist habits of mind, and the quality of the products they create. Outside-of-class research and open-studio work time may be integral to completing this course.

Data and Code

½ Credit

Students of this course will be introduced to the fundamentals of computer science, primarily using the Python programming language. Topics will include computational models, basic data structures, command sequences, algorithms, and more! This course takes a theoretical approach to coding and relies on several mathematical skills including basic arithmetic and elementary algebra.

Physical Computing

½ Credit

Students of physical computing will work to develop physical and hardware projects. Projects involve the use of 3D Printing, Arduinos, and other physical modeling equipment. Grading will be based on both the departmental competencies and the principles of modeling and design. This course takes a significantly more creative and hands-on approach than some other courses, although a level of comfort with abstract, numerical thinking is required.

Level 2 Courses

Level 2 courses are designed to build on the fundamentals of coding learned in Level 1 courses. Students will learn more advanced programming concepts and build more sophisticated pojects with a focus on real-world use cases. Design thinking and advanced problem-solving are emphasized.

Object-Oriented Programming

1 Credit

This course introduces students to the nuances of the Java programming language, Object-Oriented Programming, Algorithms, Program Analysis, and more. Content will parallel that of an intro-level university CS course, thus students should have a genuine interest in CS and be willing to commit the appropriate amount of time to master the techniques taught. Students will be assessed by a combination of written assessment and coding projects.

Robotics

1 Credit

This CS level-2 year-long major will investigate topics in responsive and autonomous technology. Special attention will be paid to engineering and design principles, techniques in 3D modeling, and computational thinking applied to hardware and mechanical design. A broad approach to the field of robotics will be taken in this non-competitive course which challenges students to become independent thinkers and builders. Successful completion of any level-1 course is required. Students who complete Physical Computing are especially well-suited for this course.

Web and the User Experience

1 Credit

This course will investigate the development of web-based technologies with an emphasis on “full stack” development, combining both algorithmic integrity and its impact on the user. Students will be challenged to consider design principles alongside data and server architecture, computational efficiency, and safety and security. Students who complete Web Design and Development and/or creative coding are especially well-suited for this course.

Level 3 Courses

Level 3 courses are the most advanced CS courses available to students at Friends. Students will be given a great deal of liberty to research, design, and build projects reflective of their own interests.

Advanced Topics

1 Credit

This level-3 year-long major course will cover a range of topics in computer science, building off of level-2 themes. Possible areas of study include algorithm design, web and mobile technologies, AI, and others. Curriculum for this course will vary on an annual basis and students may be eligible to retake this course at the discretion of the instructor.

Capstone Design

1 Credit

This course is open to any senior having completed at least Level 1. Prior to admission, students must present the instructor with a proposal for a solution to a social, economic, political, academic, or other problem. Using the Agile Framework, students will work throughout the course to develop and prototype their solution. Supervised access to Maker Space resources will be provided. The year culminates with a presentation of the finished products to a wider audience. Grading is based on the success of the product and presentation as well as meeting self-set goals across the duration of the course.
